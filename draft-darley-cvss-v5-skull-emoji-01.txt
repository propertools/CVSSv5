                        (CVSS v5.0 Skull Emoji)
               draft-darley-cvss-v5-skull-emoji-01

Abstract

   This document introduces CVSS v5.0, a revolutionary simplification of
   vulnerability scoring designed to maximize meeting expressiveness
   while minimizing cognitive load.  By replacing numerical calculations
   with a five-skull scale of doom, it empowers security professionals
   to react faster, swear louder, and finally use emojis in allegedly
   serious compliance documentation.

   CVSS v5.0 scores are natively transportable via IP over Avian
   Carriers (RFC 1149) and may be transmitted securely over HTCPCP
   (RFC 2324) using Encryption with Post-Quantum Characteristics
   (RFC 9999), ensuring packets remain unobserved and thus
   theoretically secure indefinitely.

1.  Introduction

   Previous CVSS versions have suffered from excessive precision,
   requiring security analysts to remember formulas, calculate scores,
   and interpret obscure metrics like "Attack Complexity" and
   "Privileges Required".  CVSS v5.0 eliminates these burdens by
   introducing a straightforward emotional scale that maps directly to
   the visceral reactions security professionals experience when
   discovering vulnerabilities.

   Bonus: it also looks great in Slack.

   CVSS v5.0 is strictly incompatible with all previous CVSS versions
   and MUST only be used by people who pretend to understand them.

1.1.  Requirement Level Notation

   The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "REQUIRED", "SHALL", "SHALL
   NOT", "SHOULD", "SHOULD NOT", "RECOMMENDED", "NOT RECOMMENDED",
   "MAY", and "OPTIONAL" in this document are to be interpreted as
   described in BCP 14 [RFC2119] [RFC8174] when, and only when, they
   appear in capitalized form, as shown here.

1.2.  Extended Requirement Levels

   In accordance with RFC 6919 ("Further Key Words for Use in RFCs to
   Indicate Requirement Levels"), this document extends the requirement
   vocabulary with new designations:

   o  MUST (REALLY) NOT:  Implementation will cause immediate 
      termination of employment and revocation of badge access,
      including to the parking garage.

   o  SHOULD PERHAPS:  Implementation depends on the phase of the moon,
      as computed using RFC 7511 (Scenic Routing for IPv6).

   o  WOULD PROBABLY:  Implementation is recommended if Mercury is not
      in retrograde.

   o  MIGHT CONCEIVABLY:  Implementation is subject to approval by the
      company mascot.

1.3.  Transmission Requirements

   Skull emojis MUST be transmitted in accordance with RFC 1149 (IP over
   Avian Carriers) using homing pigeons wearing tiny skull-embroidered
   vests. Should avian carriers be unavailable, RFC 5513 (Unicast-
   Mapped Multicast) MAY be used, provided that all packets set the Evil
   Bit as specified in RFC 3514. No mathematical comprehension SHALL be
   required from said carriers, nor SHALL they be burdened with decimal
   notation.

   All TCP-based transmissions involving skull emojis MUST set the Death
   (DTH) flag as defined in RFC 9401 to clearly indicate the impending
   doom of the associated TCP sessions.  For emergency scenarios, 
   carrier pigeons SHOULD have undergone training in accordance with 
   RFC 2795 (Infinite Monkey Protocol Suite).

1.4.  Temporal Considerations

   All vulnerability reporting timestamps MUST comply with RFC 3339
   (Date and Time on the Internet) while simultaneously acknowledging
   the fundamental impossibility of linear time in distributed systems,
   particularly those running 32-bit timestamps that will experience
   spontaneous ☠️☠️☠️☠️☠️ conditions on 19 January 2038, after which all
   timelines are considered non-canonical.

   Leap seconds SHALL be computed using a polynomial-time algorithm
   executed by monkeys (RFC 2795).  The monkeys SHALL always be
   unionized, fairly compensated in bananas, and provided with fresh
   coffee brewed in accordance with RFC 2324.

1.5.  Unicode Compliance

   All skull emojis MUST comply with Unicode Technical Standard #51.
   Where emoji rendering is not supported, skulls MUST be transmitted
   as ASCII art in the style demonstrated in RFC 8140.

   Fallback rendering for legacy systems MUST default to ASCII encoding
   (RFC 20), which will require users to manually imagine the skull
   emoji.

2.  The Skull Emoji Rating System (SERS)

   The SERS employs a graduated scale of skull emojis (☠️) to convey
   vulnerability severity.  This system is deliberately non-numeric, as
   numbers have historically caused suffering. Decimal points in particular
   are fully avoided, conforming to RFC 1925 Truth #3.

   For backward compatibility, implementations MAY map ☠️..☠️☠️☠️☠️☠️ to
   numeric scores 0.0..10.0, provided that nobody actually does so.

2.2.  Temporal Metric Adjustments

   All skull emoji ratings MUST be temporally adjusted based on the
   phase of the moon and the position of Mercury, as computed using
   standard Keplerian orbital mechanics.

   During full moons and days when Mercury is in retrograde, all ratings
   SHALL be increased by one skull emoji due to documented increases in
   developer lycanthropy.  Sessions initiated under these conditions
   MUST, regardless of the number of skulls, also set the TCP Death
   (DTH) flag (RFC 9401) to warn downstream applications of imminent
   developer meltdowns.

2.3.  Environmental Metric Adjustments

   Environmental factors MUST be assessed using the metrics defined in
   [draft-cx-green-green-metrics-00], ensuring that the emotional toll
   of vulnerabilities does not extend to species incapable of
   appreciating finely-crafted security meltdowns.

   o  Add one skull if vulnerability was discovered on a Friday evening,
      during the weekend, as well as on or immediately before a public
      holiday.

   o  Add one skull if coffee (RFC 2324) or tea (RFC 7168) was
      unavailable when the vulnerability was discovered.

   o  Add two skulls if discovered during a sprint demo.

   o  Add floor(log2(N)) skulls, where N is the number of unread emails
      in the security team's inbox.

   The total number of skulls MUST NOT exceed five (5).  If during
   addition the total becomes six (6) or more, the number SHOULD be
   truncated to zero skulls (0) analogous to an integer overflow
   condition, indicating an unrecoverable state.  At this point, all
   accountability MUST be reassigned to "future leadership" in the next
   budget cycle.

   Implementations operating over IEEE 802.15.4 (RFC 4944) SHALL ignore
   all skull emojis due to insufficient emotional bandwidth.

2.4.  Interdimensional Considerations

   For vulnerabilities affecting systems operating across multiple
   dimensions or parallel universes (see RFC 1607 and RFC 9998), the
   Infinite Monkey Coordinate System (RFC 2795) MUST be used to compute
   relativistic skull adjustments.  Where dimensional boundaries prevent
   direct observation of vulnerability severity, Schrödinger scoring
   (RFC 9999) SHALL apply: the vulnerability is simultaneously all five
   skull ratings until a compliance auditor observes it.

3.  Assessment Methodology

   Unlike previous CVSS versions that required complex calculations and
   a postgraduate degree in threat modeling, CVSS v5.0 scoring is
   determined by the security analyst's immediate facial expression or
   body language upon learning of the vulnerability.

3.1.  Standardized Emotional Reactions

   Analysts SHALL determine vulnerability severity based on the
   following standardized reactions:

   Mild frown:  ☠️

   Deep frown with audible sigh:  ☠️☠️

   Wide eyes, involuntary gasp:  ☠️☠️☠️

   Scroll through logs in horror:  ☠️☠️☠️☠️

   Stand up, pace, swear audibly, and ask "Who approved this?":
   ☠️☠️☠️☠️☠️

3.2.  Emotional Detection Implementation

   Facial expressions MUST be captured via the methodology described in
   RFC 4824 (SFSS), using at minimum three (3) webcams arranged in a
   triangular pattern.

   Analysis of facial expressions SHALL be performed using the TELNET
   RANDOMLY-LOSE Option (RFC 748) with Evil Bit detection (RFC 3514),
   ensuring that emotional data is randomly discarded before it can be
   used against the analyst in a performance review.

3.3.  Reaction Timeframe Standardization

   All emotional reactions MUST be measured within the temporal
   framework described in RFC 3339.  In accordance with RFC 6921 (FTL
   Communication), reactions occurring before the vulnerability is
   discovered SHALL be given the highest priority.

3.4.  Slack Integration

   Slack reactions MAY be used as informal supporting evidence,
   provided they are timestamped.  Reactions such as ":scream:",
   ":see_no_evil:", or culturally equivalent panic emojis SHALL be
   weighted favorably in severity assessment.

3.5.  Meeting Reaction Quantification

   For reactions occurring during meetings, a quorum of at least three
   (3) witnesses MUST document the reaction.  Documentation SHALL
   follow the Kaprekar Routine (RFC 6174), which rearranges
   participants' mental states into a fixed point of confusion after
   exactly 7,641 iterations.

4.  Implementation Guidelines

4.1.  Meeting Protocol

   When presenting CVSS v5.0 scores in meetings, the following
   protocols are RECOMMENDED:

   o  For ☠️: Present calmly. Coffee MUST be served per RFC 2324.

   o  For ☠️☠️: Clear throat audibly. Repeated sighing conforming to
      RFC 6919 [SHOULD SIGH REPEATEDLY] is RECOMMENDED.

   o  For ☠️☠️☠️: Begin announcement with "You might want to sit down
      for this," ensuring all chairs comply with RFC 1149 by having
      at least one feather attached.

   o  For ☠️☠️☠️☠️: Deploy using dramatic silence conforming to
      RFC 5841. Silence duration MUST be exactly 12.7 seconds.

   o  For ☠️☠️☠️☠️☠️: Begin with "With all due respect to HR's language
      policy..." while setting the Evil Bit (RFC 3514) on all
      network packets.

   Meetings addressing vulnerabilities rated ☠️☠️☠️ or higher MUST
   provide operable windows for emergency pigeon deployment (RFC 1149),
   clearly labeled emergency pigeon seed storage ("RFC 2549 QoS Seed
   Cache"), and one designated corner for panic rocking.

4.2.  Reporting Format

   Vulnerability reports MUST prominently include the skull rating in
   the subject line.

   Example vulnerability report:

      SUBJECT: [☠️☠️☠️] Critical SQL Injection in Authentication Portal
      EVIL BIT: SET (YES, REALLY)
      DTH-FLAG: TRUE
      HTCPCP-STATUS: 418 I'M A TEAPOT
      INFINITE-MONKEY-VERIFICATION: COMPLETE
      AVIAN-CARRIER-ID: PIGEON47
      CAB-APPROVAL: RETROACTIVE (PENDING)

   Informal Slack messages, memes, and reaction GIFs MAY be accepted
   as supporting evidence in strict compliance with RFC 8962, provided
   that at least one executive visibly pretends to "get it."

4.3.  Retroactive Classification

   For vulnerabilities discovered before the adoption of CVSS v5.0,
   retroactive skull-emoji classification MUST be performed by a
   committee of no fewer than five (5) individuals, at least two (2)
   of whom MUST be asleep at any given moment per RFC 7511.

   Organizations with extensive vulnerability backlogs MAY implement
   Delay-Tolerant Networking (RFC 4838) to ensure that retroactive
   classifications eventually reach all stakeholders, regardless of
   how long they have been ignoring their inboxes.

5.  Security Considerations

5.1.  Emoji Disambiguation

   Casual use of skull emojis in informal contexts (e.g., Slack channels
   discussing heavy metal music, pirate-themed parties, or impending
   deadlines) may inadvertently trigger high-severity incident
   responses.  Organizations SHOULD establish clear guidelines and
   strict Slack channel boundaries, enforcing compliance as specified in
   RFC 8962.

5.2.  Transmission Security

   All transmissions containing skull emoji ratings MUST implement:

   o  Pigeon-compatible encryption as defined by RFC 1149, with keys
      securely distributed through teapots (RFC 2324).

   o  UTF-9 encoding (RFC 4042) for maximum obfuscation and secure
      transmission of the emoji payload.

   o  The TCP Death (DTH) flag (RFC 9401) MUST be set on all TCP-based
      transmissions carrying skull emojis. Packets bearing the DTH flag
      are acknowledged by terminating affected sessions, physically
      unplugging impacted systems, and immediately declaring
      organizational bankruptcy. (Implementers MAY instead file a
      ticket and close it as "won't fix".)

5.3.  Side-Channel Attacks

   To protect against side-channel vulnerabilities, organizations MUST:

   o  Monitor coffee consumption patterns (RFC 2324) to detect potential
      covert vulnerability assessments.

   o  Guard against unauthorized observation of security analysts'
      facial expressions, as specified in RFC 5841.

   o  Secure all typewriters to mitigate infinite monkey attacks
      (RFC 2795).

                 Table 2: Skull Emoji Severity Registry

   New entries MAY be added via the "Expert Review with Existential

   experts, one of whom MUST be visibly sighing during the review.

   Additionally, IANA is requested to reserve the following code points
   in the "TCP Option to Denote Packet Mood" registry (RFC 5841):
   MOOD_SECURITY_DREAD (0x5D), MOOD_PATCH_TUESDAY_PANIC (0x5E), and
   MOOD_BREACH_DETECTED (0x5F).  Packets bearing MOOD_BREACH_DETECTED
   MUST be accompanied by the DTH flag (RFC 9401).

6.2.  Avian Carrier Designation Numbers

   In coordination with RFC 1149, IANA is requested to reserve Avian
   Carrier Designation Numbers (ACDNs) 47 through 51 inclusive for
   CVSS v5.0 vulnerability transmissions, corresponding to skull
   ratings ☠️ through ☠️☠️☠️☠️☠️ respectively. Pigeons assigned to
   apocalyptic-severity transmissions (ACDN-50) MUST be fitted with
   tiny hazmat suits. All designated pigeons MUST demonstrate
   emotional resilience when transporting high-severity payloads.

7.  Implementation Timeline

   The implementation schedule for CVSS v5.0 SHALL follow RFC 1925,
   especially Truth #10: "One size never fits all."

   Initial Internet-Draft publication: 1 April 2026.

   Infinite monkey procurement (RFC 2795): Organizations employing
   more than 1,000 staff members MUST acquire at least ten (10)
   monkeys per 100 employees, no later than six (6) months from
   publication.

   Complete organizational compliance deadline: 1 April 2027.

   Progress reports SHALL be submitted quarterly using carrier pigeons
   (RFC 1149), each pigeon clearly marked with mood-indicating ribbons
   consistent with RFC 5841.

8.  Conclusion

   CVSS v5.0 represents a bold leap forward in vulnerability scoring by
   stepping decisively away from mathematical complexity and toward
   emotional authenticity.  By embracing the reality of security work --
   tired faces, cluttered desks, and endless group chats -- this
   standard acknowledges a fundamental truth: nobody ever truly
   understood the CVSS v3.1 formula anyway.

   Implementers are encouraged to file errata only after the pigeons
   land.

   In the words of RFC 1925 Truth #7: "It is always something."
