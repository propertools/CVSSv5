## What is this?

An April Fools RFC in the grand tradition of the IETF's annual humorous publications ‚ÄĒ
RFC 1149 (IP over Avian Carriers), RFC 2324 (HTCPCP/1.0), RFC 3514 (The Evil Bit),
RFC 5841 (TCP Packet Mood), and their distinguished kin.

CVSS v5.0 replaces the vulnerability scoring system's notoriously complex numerical
formulas with a five-skull scale of doom, natively transportable via carrier pigeon
and fully compliant with post-quantum encryption (RFC 9999, expires 19 January 2038).

It is affectionate. It is absurd. **It is, we maintain, genuinely useful.**

This is an 18-month labour of love, built with AI models, real human security
researchers from all around the world, and the conviction that humor is a legitimate
form of witness to each other's struggles.

---

## The full backstory

**March 2025.** First submitted to the IETF Independent Submissions Editor.
The RFC Editor enjoyed it. Publication deferred to 2026 for production reasons.
We kept iterating, adding jokes, weaving in prior April Fools RFCs as normative
dependencies.

**March 2026.** The Independent Submissions Editor declined with clarity and grace:

> *"Actually CVSS is the creation of another organization, and I think we should be
> making fun of ourselves, not others. It's an enjoyable read, tho."*

Fair point. Full respect. We appealed.

**Also March 2026.** Vint Cerf reviewed the rejection and said the ISE was
"totally wrong" about the tradition. Chris Gibson, Executive Director of FIRST,
said he liked it "a lot." Nick Leali (Cisco), co-chair of the CVSS SIG, said he
would be "very pleased" to see it published and would help share it with the
wider CVSS community.

We filed an appeal with the IAB.

**1 April 2026, 9am New York time.** The IAB had not responded substantively.

We published independently, as planned.

## The 2027 plan

We are treating this as a proper, if ridiculous, standards development effort.

**The goal:** address the ISE's objection by reframing the document so that
the IETF's own processes, traditions, and accumulated RFC archaeology are the
proximate target ‚ÄĒ with CVSS as *the vehicle* rather than the subject. The IAB
appeal process itself is now available as additional source material.

**The 2026 record** ‚ÄĒ the ISE rejection, the Vint endorsement, the CVSS SIG
co-chair endorsement, the FIRST ED endorsement, the IAB silence, the independent
publication ‚ÄĒ will be incorporated into the 2027 submission as
Appendix A: *A Brief History of Institutional Caution.*

**How we're working:**
- Monthly editorial working calls (open to all good faith contributors)
- Issues for proposed amendments, new jokes, and structural suggestions
- PRs for proposed text changes
